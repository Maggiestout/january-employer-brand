The companies winning on hiring right now are blitzing everywhere. They treat talent brand with the same importance as product marketing. Below are the novel things high-performing employer brands are doing.

Result: when they do cold outreach to an engineer, the engineer thinks "oh this company sounds familiar" — and is much more likely to take a call.

1

Blitz Everywhere, All at Once

The best brands don't drip — they flood. Videos, podcasts, events, partnerships, product demos: stack them in compressed windows to create ambient awareness that makes cold outreach feel warm.

2

Make the Founder the Face

A public founder narrative makes the company's ambition legible in a human voice. Both Modal and Profound's CEOs are on podcasts, LinkedIn, and X — not just their marketing team.

3

Turn Every Milestone into a Brand Moment

Fundraises, partnerships, product launches: each one becomes a coordinated, multi-channel event. Profound's $96M raise became a Fortune exclusive, Nasdaq celebration, Sequoia post, and social blitz all at once.

4

Publish the Deep, Hard Work

Technical deep-dives and original research prove the team is solving real problems. Target hires read it and think "these are the problems I want to solve." The blog becomes a recruiting engine.

5

Show Up IRL

Demo nights, conferences, happy hours, office warmings. Face-to-face moments make cold outreach feel warm — candidates recognize the name before you reach out.

6

Stack Partnerships + Customer Proof

Every new logo and co-announcement is a brand moment. Volume creates ambient awareness. Customer stories double as recruiting proof — not just sales collateral.

7

Name Your Own Category

The best companies don't compete in existing boxes. They create a new framing — like "Answer Engine Optimization" — and own the conversation before anyone else does.

8

The Whole Team Becomes the Brand

Not just the CEO. When engineers, PMs, and founders are publicly visible — posting, speaking, attending — candidates see who they'd work with before they even apply.

9

Make Customer Empathy Public

The strongest employer brands don't just market software — they make candidates feel the human importance of the work. Customer stories with real people, real stakes, and real outcomes become the most compelling recruiting asset.
